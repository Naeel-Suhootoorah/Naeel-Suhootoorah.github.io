What are waterborne diseases?

Waterborne diseases are illnessescaused by microorganisms such as bacteria, viruses, or parasites that contaminate water sources. When people consume or come into contact with contaminated water, they can become infected and develop various health problems. These diseases are typically spread through drinking contaminated water, swimming in contaminated water, or consuming food that has been washed or prepared with contaminated water.

Common waterborne diseases include:

1. Diarrhea:


A common symptom of many waterborne diseases, including cholera and giardiasis, characterized by frequent loose or watery stools.

2. Cholera:


Caused by the bacterium Vibrio cholerae, cholera leads to severe diarrhea and dehydration, potentially resulting in death if left untreated.

3. Typhoid fever:


Caused by the bacterium Salmonella typhi, typhoid fever can lead to high fever, weakness, stomach pain, and severe headache.

4. Hepatitis:


A viral infection that affects the liver, hepatitis A can cause fever, fatigue, nausea, abdominal pain, and jaundice.

5. Cryptosporidiosis:


Caused by the parasite Cryptosporidium, this disease leads to diarrhea, stomach cramps, nausea, vomiting, and fever.

Prevention measures

1. Ensure access to safe and clean drinking water.
2. Practicing good hygiene, such as washing hands with clean water and soap.
3. Treat water from protentially contaminated sources before consumption.
